Your 100 Cup Roadmap

Five phases. Five target groups. One mission — make Dark Fire Security the name every tradesperson, farmer, and small business in Middle Tennessee calls first.

Your Core Script — Memorize This

"I help small businesses in Middle Tennessee protect themselves from cyber attacks — the kind that have been shutting down farms, plumbing companies, even churches. I do a free 30-minute security checkup to show exactly where you're exposed. Do you know anyone who could use one of those?"

1

Warm Network — People Who Already Trust You

Cups 1–20 · Weeks 1–2 · Practice your pitch, earn your first referrals

Cups 1–20
Who to Call First
Church members Pastor / staff Former landscaping clients CDL / trucking contacts VUMC coworkers Neighbors Family friends Emily's network
Conversation Script
"Hey, I'm launching a cybersecurity consulting business called Dark Fire Security. I'm doing 100 coffee meetings to spread the word. Would you be up for grabbing coffee? I'd love to hear what you've been up to and share what I'm building. And honestly — if you know any business owners who might need help protecting their data, I'd love an introduction."
Goal by Cup 20
Practice your pitch until it's effortless. Get at least 3 referrals to strangers. Don't sell — just plant seeds. Every cup here is a warm-up rep before you enter cold territory.
2

Local Business Owners — Main Street & Chambers

Cups 21–40 · Weeks 3–4 · Find pain points, offer free audits

Cups 21–40
Who to Target
Chamber of Commerce events Restaurant owners Retail shop owners Auto shops Salons & spas BNI groups Local Facebook business groups
Hook for This Crowd
"Small businesses are actually the #1 target for hackers right now — not big corporations. Big companies have security teams. Small ones don't. One phishing email can cost a business owner like you $50,000 or more. I help prevent that. Can I buy you a coffee and show you what I'd look for in a business like yours?"
Offer at the End of Every Cup
Offer a free 30-minute security checkup — no strings attached. Look at their email setup, Wi-Fi, and software on a simple checklist. This is your foot in the door. A few of these become your first paid clients.
3

Tradespeople & Farmers — Your Differentiated Market

Cups 41–60 · Weeks 5–6 · Where your blue-collar background is the pitch

Cups 41–60
Who to Target
Plumbers & electricians HVAC contractors Farmers & ag operations Farm co-ops & supply stores Livestock auction houses Roofers & general contractors Landscapers (you know this world)
Your Unique Angle — Use It
"I grew up blue collar — ran my own landscaping business, drove CDL. I'm not some tech guy in a suit trying to sell you something you don't understand. I'm from your world. And I want to make sure that when a hacker tries to drain your business account or lock up your files, you don't lose everything you've worked for."
Where to Find Them
TSC and farm supply stores, county fair booths, livestock auctions, contractor supply houses, county extension meetings. Your CDL and landscaping background is more valuable than any certification here — it builds trust in 60 seconds that a city consultant can't build in 60 minutes.
4

Professional Referral Partners — The Multipliers

Cups 61–80 · Weeks 7–8 · One CPA equals 50 small business clients

Cups 61–80
Who These Are
CPAs & accountants Independent insurance agents Business attorneys Commercial bankers Non-competing IT firms Managed service providers
Why This Phase Matters Most
"When your business clients ask you about cyber liability insurance — or when you notice their financial data might be at risk — do you have someone you trust to refer them to? I'd like to be that person. I'll take great care of your clients and always send them back to you happy."
The Math on This Phase
One CPA with 50 small business clients who refers you 10% of them is 5 warm leads per year — indefinitely. Land 4 professional referral partners in this phase and you have a self-sustaining pipeline that doesn't require cold outreach anymore.
5

Online-to-In-Person — Convert Digital to Real

Cups 81–100 · Weeks 9–10 · Turn followers into real relationships

Cups 81–100
Who to Convert
LinkedIn connections who engaged Local Facebook group members darkfiresecurity.com leads People who reacted to your content Newsletter subscribers
Outreach Script
"Hey [Name] — I've noticed you're building something solid in [area]. I'm doing a personal project this year called the 100 Cups Challenge — meeting 100 people in person who are doing interesting things. No agenda, no pitch — just coffee and a real conversation. Would you be up for it?"
What Cup 100 Should Look Like
By now you should have: 5+ paying clients or active proposals, 3+ referral partners sending regular leads, 1 speaking or community opportunity (a chamber talk, a church tech night, an ag extension presentation). Don't stop. Start Cup 101.
